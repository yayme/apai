The AI Paradox: Preserving Human Autonomy in an Era of Automation

From silent atrophy to Autonomy Preserving AI (APAI): balancing productivity with long-run skill retention

The problem: silent atrophy of human skill

The atrophy basin

Over-reliance on AI causes proficiency to drift past a point of no return, where independent skill cannot recover even with renewed effort.

Measured skill decline

A 2023 study found sustained GitHub Copilot users showed measurable drops in independent problem-solving ability.

Prerequisite cascades

Losing a foundational skill accelerates decay in every skill that depends on it, amplifying atrophy silently downstream.

The solution: the APAI framework

Continuous capability estimation

APAI silently monitors how the user interacts with the AI, using response patterns to estimate current skill level in real time.

Dynamic deterrent levels

When skill dips toward danger, APAI reduces AI assistance to push the user to engage more. As skill recovers, assistance relaxes.

Adaptive system prompting

The AI response shifts from complete solutions to Socratic guidance to foundational teaching, based on current skill level.
